Think and Grow Rich

"Think and Grow Rich" is a pioneering personal development and self-help book written by Napoleon Hill and first published in 1937. The book is often considered one of the best-selling books of all time, and its principles are credited with helping millions achieve success and accumulate wealth through the power of thought and positive mental attitude.

The genesis of "Think and Grow Rich" was Hill's earlier association with Andrew Carnegie, one of the wealthiest men of the early 20th century. Carnegie commissioned Hill to interview over 500 successful men and women, many of them millionaires, to discover and publish a formula for success. This project spanned 20 years and included interviews with such influential figures as Thomas Edison, Alexander Graham Bell, Henry Ford, and many others. The result was a 13-step philosophy of personal achievement, outlined in "Think and Grow Rich," which teaches individuals how to reach their goals and transform their lives.

The book emphasizes that the starting point of all achievement is desire, and it goes further to detail how to cultivate and maintain a burning desire that can help achieve any goal. Key principles include the importance of setting clear, concrete goals, continuously pursuing knowledge, organizing plans, and persisting through obstacles.
