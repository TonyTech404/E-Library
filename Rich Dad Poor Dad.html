Rich Dad Poor Dad

"Rich Dad Poor Dad: What the Rich Teach Their Kids About Money That the Poor and Middle Class Do Not!" is a groundbreaking personal finance book by Robert T. Kiyosaki with Sharon Lechter. First published in 1997, this book has since become one of the best-selling personal finance books of all time, renowned for its unconventional approach to money, investing, and wealth building.

The central premise of "Rich Dad Poor Dad" is a comparative exploration of two contrasting perspectives on money that Kiyosaki encountered growing up. The "Rich Dad," his best friendâ€™s father, is a successful entrepreneur who encourages thinking about money in terms of investment and generating passive income. The "Poor Dad," his own biological father, is a well-educated man with a stable job but traditional views about working for money. Through these contrasting figures, Kiyosaki discusses how different attitudes and teachings about money can shape one's financial destiny.

Kiyosaki uses anecdotal examples from his own life to illustrate various financial principles. One of the key lessons is the importance of financial education and understanding the difference between an asset and a liability. He stresses that true wealth is built by accumulating assets that generate income, rather than simply earning a high salary from employment.
